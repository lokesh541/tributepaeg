Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

People's president,Teacher,Space scientist

Here's the brief history of Dr kalams life

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram,TamilNadu,India
  • 1954-Graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli
  • 1955-Moved to Madras (now chennai) to study Aerospce Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1960- Mr Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO). He designed helicopters for the Indian Army, but he always said he didn’t feel at home at the DRDO.
  • 1969- I he got the Government’s approval to expand the programme by including more engineers and scientists.In 1969, Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980;
  • 1980-In the 1980s he led India’s missile programme. Under his leadership, India became a major military power after the successes of Agni and Prithvi.
  • 1981- Government of india honores him with padmabhusan third highest civilian award
  • 1990-Kalam honore with padma bhusan second higest civilian award for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.
  • 1992-He was the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.
  • 1997-Kalam received India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.
  • 1998-along with cardiologist Dr.Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost Coronary stent. It was named as “Kalam-Raju Stent” honouring them. In 2012, the duo designed a rugged tablet PC for health care in rural areas, which was named as “Kalam-Raju Tablet”.
  • 1998-the Pokhran-II tests cemented India’s nuclear prowess. Mr Kalam played the pivotal role in the project. He firmly told the international community that such arms were only to deter other nations from trying to subjugate India, and were only to be used as “weapons of peace”.
  • 2002-In a rare show of unity, all political parties unanimously voted for Mr Kalam in 2002 as the 11th President of India.
  • 2012-kalam left the presidential office office
